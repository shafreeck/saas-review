Every AI writing tool promises to "10x your content." After three weeks, five subscriptions, and
roughly 200 generated drafts, here is the honest version: they all generate text. Very few of them
generate text you would actually publish without rewriting.

This comparison tests five tools — **Jasper AI, Rytr, Sudowrite, Copy.ai, and Writesonic** —
against the same three briefs: a 1,500-word SEO blog post, a product landing page, and a
long-form research piece with citations.

## How we tested

Same brief, same day, same editor. Each tool received identical prompts with the same audience
description, tone requirements, and source material. We then measured:

1. **First-draft quality** — how much rewriting before it was publishable?
2. **Fact discipline** — did it invent statistics, quotes, or citations?
3. **Speed to useful output** — from signup to a draft worth keeping.
4. **Pricing honesty** — does the advertised price match the checkout page?

## The winner: Jasper AI

Jasper produced the only first draft that survived our edit pass mostly intact. Its brand-voice
feature is the real differentiator: after feeding it three sample articles, the output stopped
sounding like a template and started sounding like a house style.

## Best value: Rytr

Rytr is what we recommend to anyone who balks at $39/month. It scored within half a point of
Jasper on short-form copy and product descriptions, at roughly a quarter of the price. The gap
shows up in long-form structure: past 800 words, Rytr's drafts lose their thread faster than the
others.

## Who should skip AI writing tools (for now)

If your work depends on verified citations — legal, medical, financial analysis — none of these
tools passed our fact-discipline test cleanly. Every one of them invented at least one plausible
but nonexistent source during the research brief. Use them for structure and drafts, then verify
everything yourself, or don't use them for that work at all.

## The verdict

**Jasper AI** takes this round not because it writes the fanciest prose, but because it's the
most predictable: same brief in, usable draft out, every time. Predictability is what you're
actually paying for at $39/month.

If budget matters more than polish, **Rytr** is the honest choice. If you write fiction or
long-form narrative, **Sudowrite** beats both — its story-engine features are in a category of
their own.
