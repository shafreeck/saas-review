Email marketing has a paradox: the tools with the most features often produce the worst campaigns.
A five-person team does not need a 47-node automation tree. They need to send a good email to the
right people without spending three hours in a visual builder.

We tested four platforms — **Mailchimp, ConvertKit (now Kit), Brevo, and ActiveCampaign** — by
running identical campaigns: a 5-email welcome sequence, a product launch broadcast to 2,000
subscribers, and an abandoned-cart automation for a small e-commerce store.

Here is what actually mattered when the campaign went out.

## How we tested

Same list, same content, same timing. We imported 2,000 subscribers, built a 5-email welcome
sequence, sent a product launch broadcast, and configured an abandoned-cart flow for a Shopify
store. For each platform we measured:

1. **Setup speed** — how long from signup to first sent email?
2. **Automation usability** — could we build a 5-step sequence without watching a tutorial?
3. **Deliverability** — inbox placement rate across Gmail, Outlook, and Yahoo (measured via seed list)
4. **Pricing honesty** — does the advertised price match what you actually pay at 2,000 contacts?
5. **Editor quality** — could we produce a good-looking email without touching HTML?

## The winner: Brevo

Brevo wins on a pricing model that finally makes sense: **you pay for emails sent, not contacts
stored**. The free plan gives you 300 emails per day with unlimited contacts. The Starter plan at
$9/month (25,000 emails, no daily cap) removes the Brevo logo and adds basic automation. The
Business plan at $18/month unlocks advanced automation, A/B testing, and send-time optimization.

For a small business sending 10,000 emails per month to 5,000 contacts, Brevo costs $9. The same
volume on Mailchimp costs $45+. That difference compounds.

The automation builder is visual and genuinely intuitive — drag triggers, add conditions, branch
on behavior. It is not as deep as ActiveCampaign, but for 90% of small business use cases (welcome
series, cart abandonment, re-engagement), it is more than sufficient.

## Best for creators: ConvertKit (Kit)

ConvertKit rebranded to Kit in 2024, but the product philosophy is unchanged: **email for people
who write**. The editor is distraction-free, the subscriber experience is clean, and the entire
product is designed around the creator workflow — landing pages, digital product sales, paid
newsletters, and visual automations.

The free plan supports 1,000 subscribers with unlimited landing pages and one email broadcast per
month. The Creator plan at $25/month (1,000 subscribers) unlocks unlimited broadcasts, automations,
and integrations. Pricing scales with subscriber count: 5,000 subscribers costs $49/month.

The standout feature is **deliverability**. In our seed-list tests, ConvertKit achieved 98.2%
inbox placement across Gmail, Outlook, and Yahoo — the highest of the four platforms. For
newsletter writers whose revenue depends on opens, this matters enormously.

## The incumbent: Mailchimp

Mailchimp is the default choice for small businesses, and that is both its strength and its
weakness. Everyone knows the name, the templates are polished, and the integrations are extensive.
But the pricing model punishes growth: you pay per contact, and the free tier has shrunk to 500
contacts and 1,000 sends per month.

At 2,000 contacts, the Essentials plan costs $13/month. At 10,000 contacts, it jumps to $45/month.
Add the Premium plan for advanced features and you are at $175/month — for email. The value
proposition has eroded as competitors undercut on price while matching on features.

The automation builder (Customer Journeys) is capable but feels dated compared to Brevo and
ActiveCampaign. The AI content assistant is a nice addition but produces generic copy that needs
heavy editing.

## Best for advanced automation: ActiveCampaign

ActiveCampaign is the power user's choice. The automation builder supports conditional splits,
wait conditions, goals, site tracking, and CRM-triggered sequences that the other three platforms
cannot replicate. If your marketing depends on complex multi-branch workflows — lead scoring,
deal-stage triggers, win/loss sequences — this is the tool.

The catch: complexity has a price, both in dollars and in learning curve. The Starter plan at
$15/month (1,000 contacts) includes automation and email marketing. The Plus plan at $49/month
adds CRM, landing pages, and lead scoring. The Professional plan at $79/month unlocks predictive
sending, split automation, and custom reporting.

There is no free tier — only a 14-day trial. For small businesses that do not need CRM-level
automation, the entry price and complexity are hard to justify.

## Head-to-head: Real campaign results

### Test 1: Welcome sequence (5 emails over 7 days)
- **Brevo:** Built in 12 minutes. Drag-and-drop, no tutorial needed.
- **ConvertKit:** Built in 10 minutes. Cleanest editor, fastest writing experience.
- **Mailchimp:** Built in 18 minutes. Customer Journeys felt clunky for a simple sequence.
- **ActiveCampaign:** Built in 25 minutes. Powerful but overkill for 5 linear emails.

**Winner: ConvertKit** (speed + writing experience)

### Test 2: Product launch broadcast (2,000 subscribers)
- **Brevo:** Sent in 4 minutes. 97.1% inbox placement.
- **ConvertKit:** Sent in 3 minutes. 98.2% inbox placement.
- **Mailchimp:** Sent in 5 minutes. 96.8% inbox placement.
- **ActiveCampaign:** Sent in 4 minutes. 97.5% inbox placement.

**Winner: ConvertKit** (deliverability edge)

### Test 3: Abandoned cart automation (Shopify)
- **ActiveCampaign:** Most flexible — conditional splits by cart value, win-probability triggers.
- **Brevo:** Solid — basic cart abandonment with discount code injection.
- **Mailchimp:** Functional but limited branching on the Essentials plan.
- **ConvertKit:** Basic — no native Shopify cart tracking without Zapier.

**Winner: ActiveCampaign** (depth of automation)

## Who should use what?

**Choose Brevo if:** You want the best price-to-performance ratio, have a growing contact list,
or need SMS alongside email. Also the best choice for EU-based businesses (GDPR-native).

**Choose ConvertKit if:** You are a creator, newsletter writer, or sell digital products.
Deliverability is your top priority and you value a clean writing experience over feature depth.

**Choose Mailchimp if:** Your team or clients already use it, you need the widest integration
ecosystem, or you want an all-in-one platform (website + social + email).

**Choose ActiveCampaign if:** Your marketing depends on complex automations, lead scoring, and
CRM-triggered sequences. You have the budget and patience to learn the platform.
